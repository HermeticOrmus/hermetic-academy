# Communication Playbook: Tone, Language, and Visual Guidelines

**Purpose:** Define how Hermetic Academy communicates across all channels
**Audience:** 11-18 year old gaming-fluent youth
**Approach:** Authentic, competent, respectful, never cringe

---

## Core Communication Principles

### 1. **Respect Their Intelligence**
They're not children. They've mastered complex games. Speak to their competence.

**DO:**
- Assume they understand gaming concepts
- Use sophisticated vocabulary when appropriate
- Explain complex principles clearly (not simplistically)

**DON'T:**
- Talk down or condescend
- Over-explain obvious things
- Use "kiddie" language

---

### 2. **Authenticity Over Relatability**
Don't try to be "their friend." Be real, be useful, be trustworthy.

**DO:**
- Admit when something might sound cringe
- Be transparent about our business model
- Show actual gameplay/system (not just marketing)

**DON'T:**
- Force slang to seem relatable
- Pretend to be "just like them"
- Hide commercial intent

---

### 3. **Value First, Always**
Every piece of communication should deliver genuine value.

**DO:**
- Teach something useful
- Provide actionable insights
- Respect their time

**DON'T:**
- Clickbait without delivery
- Waste time with fluff
- Make empty promises

---

### 4. **Self-Aware Humor**
Acknowledge the potential cringe. Own it.

**DO:**
- "I know this sounds like self-help BS, but hear me out..."
- "Yes, we're teaching ancient wisdom through League. Stay with me."
- Meta-commentary when appropriate

**DON'T:**
- Take ourselves too seriously
- Be defensive about criticism
- Use humor to avoid substance

---

## Tone Guidelines by Context

### **Educational Content** (Principle Explanations, Quests)
**Tone:** Clear, insightful, empowering

**Voice:** Knowledgeable guide (not guru)

**Example:**
"Mentalism means your thoughts shape your reality. Not in a 'manifest your dreams' way. In a 'your mental state determines which challenges you can handle' way. Just like in League: tilt = throw game. In life: bad mental = bad outcomes. Master your mental, master your reality."

**Why It Works:**
- Defines clearly
- Distinguishes from cringe self-help
- Uses gaming metaphor
- Practical application

---

### **Community Communication** (Discord, Comments, DMs)
**Tone:** Warm, supportive, peer-level

**Voice:** Helpful community member (not authority figure)

**Example:**
"Congrats on hitting Apprentice! That's solid progress. How are you finding the Rhythm principle quests? They can be tricky at first."

**Why It Works:**
- Celebrates wins
- Asks genuine questions
- Peer-level (not top-down)
- Supportive without being patronizing

---

### **Marketing Content** (TikTok, Instagram, Ads)
**Tone:** Confident, intriguing, valuable

**Voice:** Person sharing something genuinely cool they found

**Example:**
"So I built a self-improvement app that looks like League ranked. You earn LP for IRL leveling up. All the wisdom content is free. Yeah, it sounds weird. Here's why it works..."

**Why It Works:**
- Honest about uniqueness
- Acknowledges potential skepticism
- Clear value prop (free)
- Promises explanation (keeps watching)

---

### **System Messages** (Achievements, Notifications, UI Copy)
**Tone:** Encouraging, game-like, concise

**Voice:** The game itself (like League client)

**Example:**
"Achievement Unlocked: Mental Diff Master
You completed 7 Mentalism quests. Your mental game is leveling up.
+50 LP | +500 XP"

**Why It Works:**
- Gaming language (Mental Diff)
- Specific (7 quests)
- Reward clear
- Brief (respects time)

---

### **Help/Support** (FAQ, Troubleshooting)
**Tone:** Helpful, patient, clear

**Voice:** Competent support person who gets it

**Example:**
"Streak broke and you DID check in yesterday? That sucks. Here's how to report it:
1. Screenshot your login history
2. DM @support on Discord
3. We'll restore it within 24 hours

We know streaks matter. We'll make it right."

**Why It Works:**
- Empathizes (that sucks)
- Clear action steps
- Commits to resolution
- Acknowledges emotional investment

## Language Guidelines

### Vocabulary: What to Use

**Gaming Terms** (Use confidently):
- Rank, LP, XP, level, quest, achievement
- Mental, diff, gap, cracked, carried
- Main, grind, climb, meta
- Buff, nerf, cooldown, power spike

**Hermetic Terms** (Explain first time, use freely after):
- Principle, correspondence, polarity, rhythm
- Mentalism, vibration, cause-effect, gender
- Seeker, Apprentice, Adept, Master, Sage, Ascended, Enlightened

**Self-Improvement Terms** (Reclaim from cringe):
- Growth, mastery, transformation, skill development
- Framework, mindset, practice
- AVOID: "Hustle," "grind mindset," "manifestation"

---

### Slang: When to Use

**Rarely and Strategically:**
- Use when genuinely fits
- Only if you'd use it naturally
- Platform-appropriate (Discord vs website)

**Safe Bets:**
- "No cap" (if you mean it)
- "Hits different" (describes unique quality)
- "Main character energy" (confidence)
- "Understood the assignment" (nailed it)

**Avoid:**
- Anything you have to look up
- Anything that feels forced
- Anything outdated (you'll miss timing)

**Better Approach:**
Just use standard English competently. They respect good communication over forced slang.

- **Mentalism:** Purple (#9D4EDD) - Mind, thought, consciousness

- **Vibration:** Orange (#FF6B35) - Energy, movement

- **Rhythm:** Green (#52B788) - Cycles, nature
- **Cause-Effect:** Red (#E63946) - Action, consequence
- **Gender:** Pink/Blue (#FF006E / #3A86FF) - Masculine/Feminine

**System Colors:**
- **Gold** (#D4AF37) - Achievement, divine, success
- **Dark** (#1A1A1A) - Background, space, depth
- **Light** (#F8F9FA) - Text, clarity, highlighting

---

### Typography

**Display Font** (Headlines, Titles):
- Bold, confident, game-like
- Futuristic but readable
- Example: Inter Black, Chakra Petch Bold

**Body Font** (Content, UI):
- Clean, highly readable
- Modern sans-serif
- Example: Inter Regular, Space Grotesk

**Code/Data Font** (Stats, Numbers):
- Monospace for LP/XP displays
- Gaming aesthetic
- Example: Fira Code, Roboto Mono

---

### Visual Style Rules

**1. Dark Mode Default**
- Gaming standard
- Reduces eye strain
- Neon colors pop on dark

**2. Glassmorphism**
- Frosted glass effects
- Modern, premium feel
- Depth without clutter

**3. Neon Accents**
- Glowing borders
- Realm color highlights
- Cyberpunk-adjacent (not full cyberpunk)

**4. Sacred Geometry**
- Subtle background patterns
- Principle-themed
- Not overwhelming

**5. Motion**
- Smooth animations (no jarring cuts)
- Purposeful (not decorative)
- Respects reduced motion settings

## Content Format Guidelines

### TikTok/Shorts (30-60s)

**Structure:**
```
[0-3s] HOOK - Pattern interrupt, question, or bold claim
[3-25s] VALUE - Core insight, gaming metaphor, principle explanation
[25-30s] CTA - "Link in bio," "Try the full system," etc.
```

**Visual:**
- Text on screen (always, for no-sound viewing)
- B-roll or screen recording
- Subtitles if voiceover
- Realm color accents

**Audio:**
- Trending sound (if fits) OR
- Voiceover (clear, confident) OR
- Ambient music (non-distracting)

---

### YouTube Long-Form (10-20 min)

**Structure:**
```
[0-30s] Hook & Context
[30s-2m] What you'll learn (value promise)
[2m-18m] Deep dive (principle explanation, examples, gaming metaphors)
[18m-20m] Recap & CTA
```

**Visual:**
- High-quality screen recording (1080p min, 4K preferred)
- Talking head (optional, for connection)
- Graphics to explain concepts
- Chapter markers (YouTube feature)

**Audio:**
- Clear voiceover (good mic essential)
- Background music (subtle)
- Sound design for emphasis

## Response Templates

### Positive Feedback
"Thanks! This is exactly the kind of feedback that helps us improve. [Specific acknowledgment of their point]. We're [action we'll take]."

### Criticism/Cringe Feedback
"Fair point. We're still figuring out how to talk about this stuff without being cringe. What specifically felt off? Helps us calibrate."

### Bug Reports
"Ugh, that's frustrating. We're on it. [Specific action]. Should be fixed by [timeframe]. Thanks for the report."

### Feature Requests
"Love this idea. Adding to the list. Can't promise timeline, but it's on our radar. [Why it might take time / how it fits our roadmap]."

### General Questions
"Great question. [Clear answer]. Does that help? Let us know if you need more detail."

## A/B Testing Priorities

### Test These Hypotheses:

**Tone:**
- Casual vs Professional (hypothesis: casual wins)
- Self-aware cringe acknowledgment vs confident (hypothesis: self-aware wins)
- Gaming metaphors vs direct explanation (hypothesis: metaphors win)

**CTAs:**
- "Try it free" vs "Start at Seeker I" (hypothesis: gaming CTA wins)
- "Link in bio" vs specific promise (hypothesis: specific wins)
- Emoji vs no emoji (hypothesis: 1 emoji wins)

**Format:**
- Short-form only vs short + long-form (hypothesis: both wins)
- Memes vs straight education (hypothesis: 80/20 education/memes)
- Voice over vs text only (hypothesis: VO wins on YouTube, text wins on TikTok)

## Platform-Specific Adaptations

### TikTok
- Fastest hook (3s or die)
- Always subtitles (no-sound viewing)
- Trending sounds (when relevant)
- Vertical video only

### YouTube
- 30s hook window (more patient)
- Chapters important (skippability)
- Horizontal video preferred
- SEO in title/description critical

### Discord
- Conversational (not broadcast)
- @ mentions for specific replies
- Thread conversations (organized)
- Voice chat = most authentic

### Instagram
- Visual-first (text supports)
- Stories for daily touch
- Reels = TikTok repurpose
- Feed = high-quality only

### Twitter
- Character limit respected (no threads for everything)
- Reply-friendly (engage don't broadcast)
- Quote tweets with adds value
- Memes fit naturally here

### Website
- Clarity over cleverness
- F-pattern reading (headers, bullets)
- Mobile-first (always)
- Fast load (performance = UX)

## Measuring Communication Effectiveness

### Engagement Metrics
- **Comments** (not just likes) - Are they talking?
- **Shares** (not just views) - Would they show friends?
- **Saves** (TikTok/Instagram) - Valuable enough to return to?
- **Watch time** (YouTube) - Did they stay?

### Qualitative Signals
- "This actually makes sense"
- "I sent this to my friend"
- "This is the first time I've seen [principle] explained clearly"
- "I didn't think I'd care about this but..."

### Red Flags
- "This is cringe"
- "Trying too hard"
- "Sounds like a scam"
- Silent disengagement (high bounce rate)
